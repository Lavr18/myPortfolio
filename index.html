Projects

Master-Slave communication system for RaspberryPi

This project was my first internship, so it was a particularly memorable event for me. It happened in 2017 at the University of Edinburgh. My role was to design the system that allows to control the group of RaspberryPi computers (slaves) by giving orders to just a single one (master). To solve this, I came up with an idea to use a master as a hotspot and to comminicate to slaves (connected to the same network) by using SSH port. Right now this system is in the lab of a PhD student Valerio Giuffrida, who is using it in his PhD projects.

Web-Interface and hardware trigger for plant-phenotyping platform

My BEng Project at the University of Edinburgh was focused on development of the web-interface and the hardware-trigger for Phenotiki, an affordable system for image-based plant reasearch. This was an exciting, though challenging, time for me. I learnt how to evaluate web-frameworks, automotize tasks in Linux systems, complete the design validation, and also how to present my work properly. I'd like to thank my project superwisor Dr. Sotirios Tsaftaris and his PhD student Valerio Giuffrida for their valuable advice and patience during this project. You can find the web-interface source code here.

IR-triggered remote-controlled car

One of the most exciting courses at the University of Edinburgh was Digital Systems Laborotory, where I was programming IR-transmitter and a microprocessor-based system for remote-controlled car. The whole system was implemented on FPGA board by connecting an IR transmitter, a mouse and a monitor to it. My job was to join all the peripherals into a single system. For that, I implemented an embedded Harward architecture with 16 instructions and gave machine commands in hex to the processor. Finally, I was able to control the car by using a mouse! You can watch a video of it moving here.
